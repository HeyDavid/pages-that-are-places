A small museum  / one long night · digital art through web design

Pages that are Places.

Most of the web is documents — pages that load fast, work everywhere, and get out of the way. That is the right shape for almost everything. But occasionally the right shape is a place: a scene you sit inside for a minute before you read what is there.

This is a small museum of one long night — seven rooms, seven hours, each one a page that is a place. You arrive by falling. The sky celebrates. A fire reads you. The sea delivers. Your wandering becomes a country, a flower opens briefly, and before you leave, you look up.

how the museum works

Walk down — the night deepens with you, orbit to abyss. Enter any open room and touch everything; nothing in here is fragile. Each room hands you something to take with you — a show, a poem, a sentence, a map, a sky — most of it small enough to live in a link, so you can pass it to someone else. All seven rooms are open. Sound is optional. The lights stay low.

The first door is already open. Mind the drop — the floor of this room is ninety thousand feet down.

Click the asteroids to shatter them; click the kelp and tadpoles will come. A depth gauge keeps count. The piece this whole room is built from. Enter the piece →

You climb back out of the deep, hair somehow dry. Down the corridor a stall is glowing — shells, fuses, and one piñata nobody will explain.

№02Fuse the second hour — the sky celebrates that you made it  ·  2026 · Canvas2D + WebAudio · a game Pick a shell, draw a line anywhere on the sky — any shape — and let go. The rocket rides your line, shivering harder as the fuse runs out, then blooms: peony, willow, ring, strobe, a red-white-blue glory. The piñata bursts into tacos. Send the drone swarm, run the grand finale — then share your whole show as a single link, and whoever opens it can remix it. Enter the piece →

Down a warmer corridor, a door breathes orange at its edges. Something in there has been waiting to read you.

№03Ember the third hour — the fire gets curious about you  ·  2026 · Canvas2D + WebAudio · a poem A fire that watches you read. The lines you sit with brighten from ash to ember to gold; the ones you pass stay cold. At the bottom, the fire hands back only what you actually read — your cut of the poem, with your tempo — and your reading can travel to someone else inside a link, still warm. Come closer →

Further down, the air turns salt. The fourth room has no floor — just sand, and a tide that keeps delivering.

№04Driftmail the fourth hour — the sea starts delivering  ·  2026 · Canvas2D + WebAudio · a beach A night beach with the moon's real phase in the sky. Write one sentence, cork it, and the tide carries it out — the whole letter lives inside the link you send. Open someone's link and their bottle washes ashore on your beach: uncork it, keep the letter as a pressing, or write one back. No server. No accounts. Just tide. Walk down to the water →

Past the beach the floor dries to vellum, and the hall opens into a drafting room. A pencil waits. The map is missing because you haven't walked it yet.

№05The Cartographer the fifth hour — your steps become geography  ·  2026 · Canvas2D + WebAudio · a map A blank vellum with an honest premise: where you wander becomes a country. Terrain develops around your pointer like invisible ink — contour lines, a coastline, cross-hatched depths — until you name what you've drawn and press the map. The link you carry out regrows the same country for whoever opens it. Pick up the pencil →

A warmer room now, and a smaller one. It only does one thing, once a day, and it does it for exactly sixty seconds.

№06The Pressing the sixth hour — something opens, briefly  ·  2026 · Canvas2D + WebAudio · a flower One flower opens here each day, for sixty seconds, leaning a little toward how you hold it — then it dies, and you keep the pressing: your flower, its invented Latin name, its date. Come back tomorrow and a different one opens. You can gift today's to someone in a link; it blooms once for them, and never again. Let it open →

The last room has no walls to speak of. Just the question every museum should end on: which night would you keep?

№07That Night’s Sky the last hour — before you leave, look up  ·  2026 · Canvas2D + WebAudio · a sky Type a night that mattered — a birth, a meeting, a goodbye — and the room hangs that night's sky: the moon in its true phase, a constellation that belongs to that date alone. Name the constellation, keep the poster, or send the whole sky to someone in a link. This is the room people will open for other people. Look up →

At the end of the hall there is one more doorway, lit differently. Warmer. The room where the museum gets made.
